💡 Which Regime to Choose?
A simple rule: if your total deductions (80C + 80D + HRA + home loan interest + NPS) exceed ₹3.75 lakh, the old regime is likely better. Otherwise the new regime wins for most salaried individuals, especially those earning up to ₹12.75 lakh who pay zero tax. Use the Calculator tab above to find your exact answer.

🔑 Key Changes — Income Tax Act 2025 (Effective 1 April 2026)
• The terms "Financial Year" and "Assessment Year" are replaced by "Tax Year"
• Section numbers are renamed (e.g. 80C becomes a new section number) — but benefit limits remain unchanged
• ITR-3 and ITR-4 (non-audit) deadline extended to 31 August (from 31 July) effective FY 2026-27
• ITR-1 and ITR-2 deadline remains 31 July
• Share buyback amounts now taxed as capital gains (not deemed dividend)
• Updated ITR filing allowed up to 48 months (from 24 months) from end of assessment year
• HRA: 50% exemption now applies to 8 metro cities (added Bengaluru, Pune, Hyderabad, Ahmedabad)
Source: Finance Act 2025, Income Tax Act 1961, Income Tax Act 2025, CBDT circulars, Budget 2026. This information is for educational purposes only. Section numbers and limits are subject to change. Consult a Chartered Accountant or tax professional before making any filing decisions.
